Torsional Stiffness Testing Jig

For this project, I was assigned to design, manufacture, and use a torsional stiffness testing jig to validate the accuracy of our chassis FEA simulations. I was responsible for designing the metal plates used to mount the jig to the car's wheel hubs. We outsourced the manufacturing of the plates, assembled them, and reused 8020 metal rails to allow sliding. Ultimately, we assembled the jig, saved nearly $1000 by reusing the 8020, and successfully tested the chassis stiffness.

We applied a load to the edge of the 8020 rail, imparting a moment on the chassis. Using a dial gauge, we measured the chassis's displacement to calculate its stiffness. The first data point was disregarded as an outlier due to insufficient force, but the remaining points yielded a stiffness value within 15% of our simulation, confirming the accuracy of our FEA.

UCLA 2024 HaCK: Mars Rover Project

During UCLA's 2024 HaCK competition, my team and I were tasked with building a remotely controlled rover, operable via a website, within three days. The rover needed to read temperature, humidity, and distance data, stream live video to the website, and include a claw for picking up and moving objects. I was responsible for developing the CAD and manufacturing the rover using 3D printed and laser cut parts.

By collaborating with my teammates, we produced a rover that met most of the competition requirements. Although we didn't implement a functioning claw in time, we successfully CADed, manufactured, and tested a prototype. After navigating an obstacle course that tested the rover's capabilities and completing a design review presentation, our team placed 4th out of 13.
